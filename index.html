Ludo Game Rules

Ludo is a strategy board game for 2-6 players, where the objective is to race your pawns from start to finish according to dice rolls.

Basic Rules:

  • Each player has 4 pawns that start in their home yard.
  • To move a pawn out of the home yard, you need to roll a 6.
  • If you roll a 6, you get another turn.
  • Pawns move clockwise around the board according to the dice roll.
  • When a pawn completes a full circuit, it enters the final lane of its color.
  • To win, you must get all 4 pawns to the center of the board.

Capturing Pawns:

  • If your pawn lands on a square occupied by an opponent's pawn, the opponent's pawn is captured and returns to its home yard.
  • You cannot capture a pawn that is on a safe square (colored squares).

Safe Squares:

  • Colored squares are safe zones where pawns cannot be captured.
  • Your home yard and final lane are also safe zones.

Winning:

  • The first player to get all 4 pawns to the center wins the game.
  • Pawns must reach the center with an exact dice roll.
